## **Domain Context and Terminology**

* **Overall Purpose in Daily Work:**
  The order processing and billing system is central to daily store operations. It ensures that customer purchases are billed correctly, stock is updated immediately after sales, and management can track sales performance across multiple shops.

* **Primary Goals / Outcomes:**

  * Fast and accurate billing at the counter
  * Proper tracking of inventory across stores
  * Easy handling of discounts, returns, and exchanges
  * Clear visibility of daily and monthly sales reports

* **Key Terms Used by the Domain Expert:**

| Term     | Meaning as explained by the expert                      |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Bill     | A temporary calculation of total amount before payment  |
| Invoice  | Final bill generated after payment for records and tax  |
| Barcode  | Code used to quickly identify product during billing    |
| Stock    | Quantity of items available in the store or warehouse   |
| Return   | Product brought back by customer for refund or exchange |
| Discount | Price reduction offered during sales or promotions      |

## **Core Workflows**

### **Workflow 1: In-Store Purchase and Billing**

* **Trigger / Start Condition:** Customer brings selected garments to the billing counter
* **Steps Involved:**

  1. Cashier scans product barcodes
  2. System retrieves product prices from catalog
  3. Discounts and loyalty benefits (if any) are applied
  4. Taxes are calculated
  5. Bill is generated and shown to customer
  6. Payment is collected via cash or digital method
  7. Invoice and receipt are generated
  8. Inventory stock is updated
* **Outcome / End Condition:** Successful payment and order completion

---

### **Workflow 2: Product Return or Exchange**

* **Trigger / Start Condition:** Customer requests return or exchange with bill/invoice
* **Steps Involved:**

  1. Cashier verifies original purchase details
  2. Item condition is checked
  3. Eligibility for return is confirmed
  4. Refund amount is calculated or exchange item is selected
  5. Refund is processed or exchange completed
  6. Inventory stock is updated accordingly
* **Outcome / End Condition:** Refund issued or replacement item delivered

---

### **Workflow 3: Inventory Replenishment**

* **Trigger / Start Condition:** Stock level falls below reorder threshold
* **Steps Involved:**

  1. Inventory manager reviews low-stock items
  2. Purchase order is created
  3. Order is sent to supplier
  4. Goods are received and verified
  5. Inventory stock is updated in the system
* **Outcome / End Condition:** Stock levels restored

## **Rules, Constraints, and Exceptions**

* **Mandatory Rules or Policies:**

  * Invoice must be generated for every completed payment
  * Returns are allowed only with original bill/invoice

* **Constraints or Limitations:**

  * Dependence on third-party billing tools (Shoppers and QuickBill) limits customization
  * Lack of a fully centralized system for real-time inventory visibility across all branches

* **Common Exceptions or Edge Cases:**

  * Barcode not scanning properly for older or damaged tags
  * Special discounts applied manually for loyal or bulk-purchase customers

* **Situations Where Things Usually Go Wrong:**

  * Stock mismatch due to manual adjustments
  * Requiring manual intervention for edge cases or specific defect-related scenarios

## **Current Challenges and Pain Points**

* Physical discount labels displayed on racks often differ from discounts configured in the billing system, leading to customer dissatisfaction and disputes at the counter
* Difficult to track exact stock across multiple shops
* Manual intervention required for returns and discounts
* Reports from different systems need reconciliation
